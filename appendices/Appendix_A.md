# Appendix A. Corpus Construction and Sample Screening

This appendix documents corpus construction and sample screening.

## A.1 Retrieval windows

**Table A1. Retrieval windows**

| Wave | Role | Date range |
|------|------|------------|
| T1 | 2021 disaster period | 18 July – 7 August 2021 |
| T2 | 2025 heavy-rainfall context | 4–17 August 2025 |

T2 is defined by renewed heavy-rainfall / flooding / public-safety risk in summer 2025, not by calendar anniversary alone. T1 extends beyond peak disaster days to include short-run aftermath discourse.

**Keywords (OR-combined, both waves):** 郑州暴雨 (Zhengzhou rainstorm); 郑州洪水 (Zhengzhou flood); 郑州7·20 (Zhengzhou 7/20); 郑州内涝 (Zhengzhou urban flooding). Auxiliary commemorative query tracks are excluded from the main corpus.

## A.2 Retrieval and screening

Posts were retrieved through keyword and date-window searches, backfilled to obtain accessible status records, merged into an analysis-ready table, and screened for visibility before Gate and Core annotation.

**Table A2. Sample attrition**

| Step | Records | Exclusion / criterion |
|------|--------:|------------------------|
| Initial retrieved posts | 62,513 | Keyword and date-window retrieval; deduplicated by post identifier |
| Backfilled records | 38,980 | Available status records after backfill |
| Merged records | 39,654 | Wide-table merge and field alignment |
| Accessible records | 24,796 | Visible survivor subset (Gate input) |
| Core corpus | 17,143 | Relevant posts with high relevance score |

The apparent increase from 38,980 to 39,654 reflects field alignment during merging rather than an additional retrieval stage. Gate filtering removed 7,653 of 24,796 visible posts. Labels were harmonized across duplicate texts within each wave by majority vote.

## A.3 Corpus boundary

The main sample comprises posts that remained accessible at collection, were successfully backfilled, and passed Gate/Core screening (N = 17,143). Deleted, inaccessible, or never-retrieved posts are outside the sample. Engagement outcomes measure interaction within this survivor corpus and do not measure algorithmic exposure, recommendation intensity, or platform-wide survival probability. Accordingly, all inferences concern observable public discourse rather than the complete universe of platform activity.
